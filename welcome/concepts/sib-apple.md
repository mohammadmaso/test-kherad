> This page is derived from a Persian-language source about apples (سیب).

**سیب** (apple, scientific name: *Malus domestica*) is a cold-climate tree fruit from the Rosaceae family. It comes in several varieties including Gala apple, red apple, green apple, and wild apple. This fragrant and flavorful fruit contains significant amounts of potassium, sodium, calcium, bromine, phosphorus, and vitamins A and B.

## Nutritional Tips

### Peeling Guidance
- Most vitamins are located in the peel and just beneath it. Those who can digest apple skin are advised to eat it with the peel.
- If peeling is necessary, peel as thinly as possible to preserve nutrients.

### Pectin Content
- Apple skin contains a significant amount of **pectin** — eat apples with the skin to benefit from it.

### Washing
- Apples sold in Iranian, American, and Canadian markets are coated with a protective layer of fat. These must be washed thoroughly before consumption.

### Sensitive Stomachs
- People with weak digestion should peel apples very thinly to avoid losing pectins and vitamins.
